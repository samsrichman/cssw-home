Welcome!

Columbia School of Social Work has been a leader in social work education and research since 1898.

It joins rigorous academic theory with real-world practice to enhance the welfare of citizens and communities in New York City, the nation and around the world. In the centre, sticking into the skin of our old planet Earth like a poisoned dart, was this cylinder.

Working with a women’s group in Rwanda, Alexis Miesen Gallivan helped start an ice cream shop meant to bring healing and joy to a country recovering from civil war. Learn more

Using her skills in communication, team-building, and resource management, Amy Shapiro is working to make the field of technology more diverse and inclusive. Learn more

Desmond Patton analyzes the role of social media in precipitating real world violence. Working with experts in computational linguistics and machine learning, Patton helps social media platforms identify signs of violence and respond to de-escalate. Learn more

Professors Dr. Nabila El-Bassel and Dr. Neeraj Kaushal recently traveled to Jordan to set up a multidisciplinary, University-wide project that seeks to identify the needs of Syrian refugees in the areas of research, education, training and service. Learn more

Working in the fashion industry, Laurie Marsden saw first hand the pervasive issues women had with self-esteem, identity, and personal boundaries. Now she uses modern telecommunication platforms to offer therapy across the country to those in need. Learn more

Julie Cummings’ Lovelight Foundation is dedicated to empowering women and their children, and by assisting with the production of “I Am Jane Doe,” Cummings and the foundation helps mothers tell the story of their daughters who fell victim to sex trafficking. Learn more
